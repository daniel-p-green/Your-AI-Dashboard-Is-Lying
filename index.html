The Measurement Problem

Your AI Dashboard
Is Lying to You

The real signal is not usage. It is deletion: which reports stopped being written, which workflows collapsed, which artifacts simply vanished.

Head of Global AI, Logitech

The Setup

Three angles. Same blind spot.

Enterprise
The engineer with 40 prompts shows up on the dashboard.

The finance manager who killed 3 reports does not.
Cognitive
Addition is visible.

Nobody credits you for the ten things you said no to.
Personal
AI makes adding easy.

The real question: what do you subtract so the right things get your time?

It is deletion.

What reports stopped getting written?
What workflows collapsed?
What artifacts vanished because an agent replaced them?Eric Porres, Head of Global AI, Logitech

02 / The Core Paradox

Everyone adopted AI.
Almost nobody changed.

0%
report using AI in at least one business function. Weekly exec usage exceeds 80%. Three in four see positive returns. The adoption story looks complete.
0%
earn meaningful EBIT from it. People try the applications. They just do not change the work. Leaders misread this as an execution gap. It is a measurement gap.
The whole game is the distance between 88% and 6%.
AI adoption is not a training problem.
It is a deletion problem.

The models are good enough.
The analytics are fancy enough.
The real bottleneck: who has permission to stop doing the old thing?Eric Porres, Head of Global AI, Logitech

01
Sidecar Analytics
Mapped usage across org charts, countries, and teams. Used sunbursts for engagement tiers and ridgelines for token distributions.
Showed who experiments. Did not show who changed.
02
Behavior Over Volume
Shifted to engagement profiles: inactive, dabblers, regulars, and power users. Asked "what kind" instead of "how many."
Revealed the frozen middle. Did not fix it.
03
Outcomes and Deletion
Stopped asking "where are people using AI?" and started asking "where did work disappear?"
Analytics became receipts for deletion.
05 / Garbage Cans & Bitter Lessons

Obsess over outputs, not inputs.

The Garbage Can
Obsess over inputs
Map every messy process. Untangle every handoff. Build better tools to do old work faster. Feel mysteriously underwhelmed.
Result: faster old work. Same old work.
The Bitter Lesson
Obsess over outputs
Define "good." Feed AI examples. Let it route through chaos. Then ask: if an agent produces this reliably, what can we delete?
Result: whole artifacts vanish. Whole rituals end.

The manager band owns KPIs, templates, compliance, and career risk.

They show sporadic experimentation but very little systematic redesign.

They hold the permission to delete old work.

And they are the most pressured layer in the org.
07 / AI Pulse

Measure the pattern,
not the snapshot.

Consistency
Is AI muscle memory, or a monthly stunt?
Intensity
Are you pushing real work through it, or editing subject lines?
Creation
Are you building things that live beyond you?
Impact
Does your created work get reused, or die in drafts?
Breadth
Are you stuck in chat, or working across every tool?
Skills Floor
Have you built the baseline, or reinventing from scratch?
08 / The Working Sequence

Six steps from dashboard theater to real change.

1
Build one honest picture
Join all AI exhaust into one place.

One row per person, enriched with org context.
2
Move beyond "active"
Define behavioral segments: inactive, dabbler, regular, power user.

Not raw volume.
3
See the manager band
Slice by reporting line.

Surface the frozen middle where change stalls.
4
Tie analytics to deletion
Measure cycle time collapse.

And the absence of old artifacts.
5
Turn dashboards into conversations
Use data to spark honest, uncomfortable conversations.

About what to stop.
6
Layer on coaching
Offer specific next moves.

For individuals, teams, and leaders.

The bridge is not another model, another pilot, or another training course.

Who has permission to say the old version is done?

Your AI dashboard will not do that for you. But if you design it right, it will stop letting you pretend you already have.
